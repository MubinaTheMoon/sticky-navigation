Formation of Clouds

Evaporation: Water from oceans, lakes, and rivers evaporates into the air as water vapor.
Rising Air: Warm air rises because itâ€™s less dense.
Cooling: As the air rises, it cools. Cooler air can hold less water vapor than warm air.
Condensation: When the air cools to its dew point, water vapor condenses around tiny particles in the air (like dust, salt, or smoke) to form droplets or ice crystals.
Cloud: These tiny droplets or crystals cluster together, forming a cloud.
